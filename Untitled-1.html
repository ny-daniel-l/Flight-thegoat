Kimani Tariq Kamiru-White — better known online as FlightReacts or simply Flight — is an American YouTuber, Twitch streamer, and musician. Born in Washington D.C. and raised in West Palm Beach, Florida, he built one of the most entertaining basketball-reaction brands on the internet. He runs two major channels: FlightReacts (reaction content) and NotYourAverageFlight (gaming, basketball, and life). His passionate, unfiltered love for the NBA — especially his willingness to play actual basketball on camera despite mixed results — made him a fan favorite and a viral sensation.

From Humble Beginnings

Origin Stories

✈ The Flight Story

Born in Washington D.C., Kimani White moved to West Palm Beach, Florida as a baby — a city where basketball culture ran deep. From a young age he was performing: rapping lyrics in front of his elementary school class, soaking up the energy of Allen Iverson, Snoop Dogg, and Lil Wayne. He wanted to entertain — always.

Middle school was rough. Flight has spoken openly about being close to the wrong crowds — gangs, violence — but basketball and entertainment kept him grounded. He was cut from his high school basketball team three times, yet kept showing up. That never-give-up energy would later become his brand.

After high school, he took on two jobs in fast food to get by. He was going through a tough stretch mentally — battling anger and depression — and YouTube became his outlet. In June 2013 he launched NotYourAverageFlight, uploading NBA 2K13 videos. The energy was undeniable from day one.

💰 The Cash Story

Cassius Jeremy Clay grew up in the small town of Vidalia, Louisiana, raised by his parents Cassius Lavern Clay and Cynthia Ann Clay. Surrounded by his younger brother Jamarcus and sister Desteni, young Cash was never short on personality. He won a State Art Competition in elementary school and played football, wrestling, basketball, and baseball at Vidalia High School.

Comedy was always his thing. He idolized LeBron James on the court and made everyone around him laugh off it. He dreamed of acting or filmmaking, but life after high school meant a practical path — he skipped college and worked as an assistant manager at Walmart. A close friend eventually pushed him to put his stand-up humor online.

In June 2013, he launched a channel called WhatChaMaCalling (later renamed CashNasty). His first video was an NBA 2K13 Lakers vs. Knicks commentary — and it showed everything: the humor, the passion, and the charisma that would eventually earn him millions of fans worldwide.

The Main Event

The Rivalry

What started as two NBA-obsessed YouTubers reacting to the same content slowly evolved into one of YouTube's most beloved ongoing rivalries — not born from hatred or drama, but from something rarer: genuine competition, mutual respect, and the undeniable chemistry of two personalities who simply make each other funnier.

01

Same Lane, Different Worlds

Flight (West Palm Beach, FL) and Cash (Vidalia, LA) came from totally different backgrounds, but both launched YouTube channels in 2013 centered on NBA 2K and basketball content. As they grew their audiences, their worlds inevitably collided — two of the biggest voices in the basketball YouTube space.

02

The 1v1 Series Begins

The rivalry crystallized on the basketball court. Flight, standing 6'4" and endlessly confident in his game, invited Cash to a 1v1. The result became legendary. Cash, despite being shorter, proved he had serious game — and the internet went wild. Their first battles racked up millions of views almost instantly.

03

Cash's Dominant Record

Across four documented 1v1 matchups, Cash won every single one. In one infamous game, Cash beat Flight with one hand literally taped behind his back — a moment that became one of the most memed and replayed clips in basketball YouTube history. Flight fans howled; Cash fans never let him live it down.

04

Jabs & Online Banter

Between games, the two would throw verbal jabs online — hype-building tweets, IG stories, and reactions to each other's content. They'd react to each other's games, roast each other's plays, and fan flames just enough to keep the internet buzzing. Their banter became content in itself.

05

"June Flight" Lore

Flight developed a mythology around his game in June — claiming he rarely loses in that month. He hired trainer Ryan Razooky (R2BBall) to reach his "final form." Their June 2020 1v1 at Dune Citi gym carried a wager: if Flight won, Cash had to shave his beard. Cash won anyway — but Flight's jumper had visibly improved.

06

More Than a Rivalry

Despite the competition, Flight and Cash share genuine camaraderie. They've collabed, reacted to each other's content, and even hyped each other's milestones. Cash once joked about setting up a 1v1 between their daughters one day. This isn't just a rivalry — it's a friendship the internet gets to watch unfold in real time.
